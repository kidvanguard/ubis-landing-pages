# UBIS Google Ads Campaign Strategy

## Campaign Overview

**Goal:** Drive qualified prospects to ABD and Masters transfer landing pages at the lowest possible cost per click by targeting long-tail, high-intent keywords that competitors ignore.

**Core principle:** Avoid head terms ("MBA online," "DBA program," "masters degree"). These cost $15-50+/click. Instead, target the specific pain points and situations of people who are already past the research phase and looking for a solution.

## Campaign Settings

### Location Targeting
- **Primary:** United States
- **Secondary (separate campaigns with lower bids):** UK, Canada, Australia, Nigeria, India, UAE, Saudi Arabia
- For international, use the international keyword groups only

### Ad Schedule
- Run 24/7 initially
- After 2 weeks, review conversion data and reduce bids during low-conversion hours (typically 1am-6am in target time zones)

### Device Bidding
- Start with equal bids across devices
- Desktop typically converts better for higher-education decisions; consider +10-15% bid adjustment after data accumulates

### Audience Targeting (Observation Mode)
Add these as observation audiences to learn who converts, then bid adjust:
- In-market: "College & University"
- In-market: "Business Education"
- Affinity: "Avid Investors" (for DBA)
- Affinity: "Business Professionals"
- Custom intent: people searching for "dissertation help" or "finish my degree"

## Conversion Tracking

Set up these conversion events:

1. **Calculator interaction** — user adjusts calculator inputs (micro-conversion, high volume)
2. **CTA button click** — "Start Your Application" / "Get Free Evaluation" (primary conversion)
3. **Time on page > 60 seconds** — engaged visitor (secondary signal)

Use Google Ads conversion tracking or Google Tag Manager. The calculator interaction is your highest-volume signal and will optimize the algorithm fastest.

---

## Why This Strategy Works

1. **Long-tail keywords cost 5-10x less** than "MBA online" ($3 vs $30+)
2. **Higher intent** — someone searching "ABD completion program" is further along than "what is an MBA"
3. **Low competition** — most universities bid on head terms; almost nobody bids on "finish my dissertation" or "transfer doctoral credits"
4. **Landing page match** — each keyword group maps directly to page content, improving Quality Score and lowering CPC further
5. **Negative keywords** — aggressively filtering out freebie-seekers and researchers who will never convert
